The African Legend and founder of the Afrobeats: Fela Kuti

Fela Anikulapo Kuti (15 October 1938 â€“ 2 August 1997), also professionally known as Fela Kuti, or simply Fela, was a Nigerian multi-instrumentalist, musician, composer, pioneer of the Afrobeat music genre and human rights activist. He has been called "superstar, singer, musician, Panafricanist, polygamist, mystic, legend. At the height of his popularity, he was referred to as one of Africa's most "challenging and charismatic music performers.

He was know to be vocal and ciritical about the socio-economic injustice going on in Nigeria as well as the continent of Africa. Some of his notable features are as follow:

  • He's a first cousin to nobel laureate, Prof Wole Soyinka
  • Two of his sons are notable and accomplished musicians
  • He purposely sang in pidgin, because he wanted his message to be easily undrstood across Africa
  • He was arrested multiple times by the then Nigerian lead military government between the 70s and until his death in 1997
  • He had political ambitions to run for the president but was denied candidature, he formed a party known as M.O.P
